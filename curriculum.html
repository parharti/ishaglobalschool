A Curriculum That Transforms

Learning that goes beyond textbooks — nurturing thinkers, not just test-takers

Learning Without Limits

Our curriculum is not a rigid list of topics to be memorised. It is a living journey that respects each child's unique pace, curiosity, and learning style.

Drawing from J. Krishnamurti's philosophy, Japanese school methods, and modern experiential learning research, we prepare children for life — not just exams.

Three Pillars of Learning

The core methods that shape how children learn at IGS

Experiential & Inquiry-Based

Children learn by doing — hands-on projects, real-world exploration, and guided discovery. Every lesson invites questioning.

  • Hands-on experiments
  • Project-based assignments
  • Student-led discussions
  • Real-world problem solving

Japanese Methods

Inspired by Japan's world-renowned primary education system — discipline without fear, independence with responsibility, joy of learning.

  • Morning reflection routines
  • Classroom responsibility
  • Group harmony & cooperation
  • Intrinsic motivation

Emotional Intelligence

EQ is not a subject — it is a way of being. Children learn to understand emotions, empathise, and navigate challenges with resilience.

  • Daily reflection activities
  • Conflict resolution skills
  • Empathy circles
  • Emotional vocabulary building

Smart Class Integration

Every classroom at IGS is equipped with smart boards and digital learning tools — making abstract concepts vivid, interactive, and memorable.

Our online platform gives parents real-time progress updates and direct communication with teachers — anytime, anywhere.

Teacher as Facilitator

IGS teachers are guides, mentors, and co-learners. They walk alongside children as they discover — they don't stand in front and pour information.

Trained to guide curiosity rather than enforce conformity, they build deep relationships with each student — understanding individual strengths and gently nurturing growth without labels or ranks.
